C1.L3.1 - etraSa -- stem: "etr(a)+", suffix: "Sa" (cf. trutanaSa). ?"verb"?, ?"adjective"?. Appears in similar form on 5.c.BC. bronze sheet from Cerveteri: "eta.0esan.etras.uniia0i.{missing}.hutilatina.etiasas.acalia.{missing}.0anaXvilus.ca0arnaial" Glossed as: "This Thesan (Aurora)(daughter) of Etra (Gr. Aithra) in the sanctuary of Uni; during the fourth day etiasa (Idus?) of/in June (possible) of Thanachvil Catharnaia" Appears also in a lead sheet from Vulci (ca. 450-400 BC.): "uni.cel.etra.{missing}.uSesl.nva.ser.{missing}" Theonyms appear: Uni, Cel, Etra, and Usil. etraSa may appear in different forms: e0rsi,e0rse (C2.n2), e0ri (11.3), etrin0i (12.5). Possibly related to etera, a place in a tomb (C10.22). Personally, as it appears in contexts of birth (Uni, Cel), light (Aurora, Usil), and, overarching, an emergence, I find it to be a lexical unit denoting something within something, or something emerging from something. Possibly a ritual specific term.

C1.L4.1 - ziXri, same as 1.1

1.4.2-3 - cn.0unt, cn: "this", not dependent on ziXri. 0unt may be a participle of verb 0un (to do first, firstly). The syntagm might be similar to cntnam.0esan: do the same during the morning / during the same morning. It is unclear if 0unt has any relation to 0un.lurs0 (firstly, in the area of Lur{theonym}). Might be indicative of the gall bladder as a similar syntagm appears on the Piacenza Liver.
